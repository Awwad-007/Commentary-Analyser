The second El Clásico of the year took place on October 26, 2024, and proved to be a one-sided affair in the second half. Real Madrid struggled to break the defensive line, with Kylian Mbappé finding himself flagged for 
offside on two separate occasions in the first half. The momentum shifted entirely after the break when Robert Lewandowski struck twice in three minutes to stun the home crowd, first with a clinical finish from Marc Casado's
through ball and then with a soaring header from an Alejandro Balde cross. Despite missing an opportunity for a hat-trick when he hit the post, Lewandowski’s impact opened the floodgates. Lamine Yamal added a third in the
77th minute with a powerful strike into the top corner, and Raphinha finalized the rout in the 84th minute by lofting the ball over the goalkeeper to seal a dominant 4-0 win for Barcelona.
